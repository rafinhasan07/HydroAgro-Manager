How this aquifer model works

The simulator solves a simple groundwater balance each month from January 2000 to December 2025: H(t+1) = H(t) + R(t) − α · P(t) − β · (H(t) − Heq), where H(t) is groundwater level, R(t) is seasonal recharge, and P(t) is pumping demand.

Recharge follows a sinusoidal pattern R(t) = 0.4 · sin(2πt / 12), representing wetter and drier seasons, while pumping is set by the pumping slider and scaled by seasonal irrigation demand P(t) = PumpSlider · max(0, sin(2π(t−3)/12)), with higher use in summer.

The parameters α = 0.02, β = 0.08, and Heq = 10 control how strongly pumping removes water and how quickly the aquifer relaxes back toward its equilibrium level. Use the sliders to see how sustained high pumping can deepen groundwater levels over years, and how lower rates allow the system to recover toward equilibrium.
